    I’m a creative technologist and multidisciplinary designer.

    I've been studying for three years at the Design and Technology program at Parsons School of Design. My background has played an integral role in developing a mission of creating thoughtful work that helps people and serves to make meaningful stories accessible. I’ve learned heavily from working with non-profits and local businesses in New York City and Montgomery, Alabama that fight towards greater education equity and climate advocacy. In my work, I focus on interaction design, physical computing, and real-time visualizations. In my free time, I like to create graphic art posters using typography and geometric forms, paint portraits of humans and animals, and play the flute, guitar, or piccolo.

    Core Values

    Welcome inspiration.

    My goal is to use design to connect concepts, cultural moments, and people in a compelling way. I tend to look for inspiration from observation, conversation, and formal design research to stretch my perspective.

    Be empathetic.

    I consider how my product will intervene in the system its a part of, as well as how it will affect the people within that system. I remember that others experience the world differently and ask how I can make my products most acessible.

    Embrace experimentation.

    I strive to branch out in my work and make new discoveries with every step of the design process. I try to always stay curious and allow myself to learn new and more meaningful ways to solve problems that help me turn my ideas into real experiences.

    Build relationships.

    Collaboration and teamwork is essential for good design. By taking the time to build relationships with our teammates and clients, we can more efficiently create solutions.

    Have any thoughts, comments or ideas? Let’s connect :)
